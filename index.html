The Night before..


The Night before will make or break your day off. Here's some things that'll lead you to success.

Dinner Shower/Bath Sleep
Treat yourself with a meal that you've been craving.
Here are some of my recommendations:
  1. Eat something that's associated with a good time; e.g. if you can, hit up your childhood pizza spot.
  2. Eat something familiar. For example, anything that brings you joy within the first bite.
  3. If you have the ingredients, make some pesto pasta(so good).
  4. And of course, stay hydrated.
 Enjoy a nice steamy shower or bubble bath. The choice is yours. I reccomend lavender,coconut, or vanilla scents for tonight. Make sure to oil your skin and/or add your favorite lotions. Enjoy a nice cup of warm matcha while reading your favorite book. Don't scroll on social media. It doesn't serve you.
Doze off to ambient music (432hz is my favorite) or a relazing meditation.

The Morning Of..

Good morning my love! Your morning is going to be light and easy. Start off by sitting in an upright position on your bed,
legs criss-cross applesause, palms facing up, and eyes closed. Focus your energy twords your breath and breathe normally.
After a few inhales and exhales, start changing it up using some of the excersises down below. Brething is my favorite way to
cope with stress, bring me back to reality, and remind me that I am human. Such a simple thing heals.
Next, whip up some eggs and start your omelette.

I want you to journal while thats cooking and set inntentions for the day. This is important because you
don't want to slack and repeat the same cycle of stress that you've been trying to get out of.

Now that you have a plan, I'm going to be 100% honest with you. Get things done today. Please. The porodomo technique is so useful
for those who struggle with procastination.

Preparing For The Next Day..

Around 8 pm, you need to start settling down. Since you've already completed what needed to be done,
it's time to stay ahead of the game.

"Getting ready" doesn't only apply to the morning. It starts now.

  • Prepare an outfit that makes you feel your best (make sure its
    comfortable)
  • Meal prep. (Overnight oats are quick, nutritious, and delicious. They'll
    get you through the first half of the day. Make sure to pack a luscious salad with
    the right dressing to match)
  • Skincare!!!! This is soooo important. You face is the first thing people look at.
    (Incorporate facial massages, steam, and ice the face for maximum effects.
  • Read a book. (You don't want your last moment of your beautiful day to be spent on your phone.
    Reading a book will spark your imagination and calm your nerves for your long day tomorrow.)



After you've completed all those steps, here's my advice. GO. TO. SLEEP. No matter how ready
you think you are, nothing will be worth it if you're tired and sluggish. I promise.

Hopeflly by now, you're ready to send that email or call out sick. You deserve that day off and
Now you know how to tackle it!:)
